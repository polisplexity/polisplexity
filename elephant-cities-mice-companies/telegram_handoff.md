Hey! Cities do not scale like companies. And that difference matters much more than it seems.

Back in 2021, I gave a talk around a question that still feels very relevant to me: why can cities become more productive, diverse, and complex as they grow, while many companies become more rigid, administrative, and less adaptive?

The core idea is simple: scaling does not mean the same thing across every system.

In a city, growth can multiply encounters, specialization, learning, conflict, and innovation. In a company, growth can also multiply coordination costs, hierarchy, friction, and loss of flexibility.

I am republishing this talk as a living archive, with the original slides and an enriched public narration. Not as academic nostalgia, but because the question is still strategic for urban policy, organizations, and sustainability: https://polisplexity.github.io/polisplexity/elephant-cities-mice-companies/

My position: we should not govern cities as if they were large companies, and we should not design companies as if they were small cities.

What should we learn from cities to build more adaptive organizations?
